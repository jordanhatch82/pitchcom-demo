# A Pitch-Calling App for Coaches — Concept for Your Feedback

*Working idea — I'd genuinely love your honest take. Questions at the bottom.*

---

## The problem

Calling pitches and plays from the dugout is clunky. Hand signals and wristbands work, but kids miss them, the other team steals them, and there's a lot of "look at me… no, look at me" before every pitch. I wanted something faster, clearer, and harder to swipe.

## The idea

**Call the pitch or play from your phone, and it shows up instantly on your players' Apple Watches.**

You tap what you want on your phone. A half-second later, the right players feel a buzz on their wrist and see a big, easy-to-read display telling them exactly what's coming. No squinting at the third-base coach, no decoder wristbands.

## What it looks like for you (the coach)

On your phone, between pitches:

- Tap one of your **favorite calls** (like "Curveball, low and away") — one tap and done.
- Or build it yourself: tap the **pitch**, tap the **spot** on a strike-zone grid, hit **Send**.
- Separate tabs for **plays** (pitchout, pickoff, bunt defense) and a quick **"where's the play"** — tap a base and your fielders know where the ball's going.

You also get a little green check showing **how many watches got the call**, so you know it landed.

## What your players see

The display is tailored to where they play, so nobody's staring at info that isn't theirs:

- **Pitcher & catcher** — the pitch and the exact location, big and bold.
- **Infielders** — a little diamond showing the play (e.g. "pickoff at 2nd").
- **Outfielders** — where to position ("no doubles — play deep").

Their wrist buzzes when something new comes in, so they're not babysitting the watch — they just glance when it taps them.

## How it connects

The watches get the call over the internet (their own cell signal, or a wifi hotspot you bring in your bag). It's nearly instant. The only spot it struggles is a field with truly *zero* signal for anyone — rare, and something I have ideas for down the road.

## Managing your team

Before the game, in the app, you'd set up:

- Your **roster** and who's playing where
- Each **pitcher** and which pitches they actually throw
- Your **go-to calls** as one-tap favorites

Then on game day you just hit **Start Game** and go.

## What this is *not*

- It's **not** deciding whether your league allows electronic devices on the field — that's on the coaches to check and decide.
- It's **not** a scorekeeping app. It won't track balls/strikes/outs for you — it just sends the calls.
